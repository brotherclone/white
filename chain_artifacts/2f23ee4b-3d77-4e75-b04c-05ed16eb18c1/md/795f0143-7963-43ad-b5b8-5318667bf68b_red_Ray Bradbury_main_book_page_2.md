The children were the first to understand. Little Tommy Brennan, who lived in the blue house with the crooked chimney, came running to me that evening with eyes wide as saucers.

"Mr. Spaulding," he whispered, "they're stealing the quiet!"

I knelt down to his level, smelling the summer dust in his hair. "What do you mean, son?"

"The spaces," he said, spreading his small fingers wide. "The spaces between the radio songs, between the TV shows, between when Mama talks and when she listens. They're taking all the spaces and putting them in jars."
