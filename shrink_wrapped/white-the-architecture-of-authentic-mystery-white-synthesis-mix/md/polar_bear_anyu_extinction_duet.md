# Polar bear / Anyu Kanguq

## Species Arc: Polar bear
Population decline from 25,000 (2001) to functional extinction (2100). Sea ice habitat loss averaging 13% per decade. Denning sites flooded by rain-on-snow events. Cubs drowning during extended swims between ice floes. Starvation as seal hunting grounds disappear. Final captive populations in zoos unable to breed successfully.

## Human Arc: Anyu Kanguq
Traditional knowledge keeper watching her world dissolve. Morning vigils at kitchen window. Grandfather's tools becoming museum pieces. Government notices and relocation pressure. The weight of being witness to cultural extinction. Swimming toward nothing.

## Parallel Moments

### 1. Seventy years before extinction
**Species:** 25,000 polar bears patrol stable sea ice highways, cubs learning to hunt on platforms that have existed for millennia

**Human:** Anyu's grandfather carves ice-reading symbols into bone tools, teaching her that ice speaks if you know how to listen

*Connection: Foundation knowledge passed through generations - both species and culture built on ice's reliability*

### 2. Thirty years before extinction
**Species:** 8,000 bears remain, forced to swim longer distances between shrinking ice floes, cubs drowning during extended ocean crossings

**Human:** Anyu watches her children leave for southern cities, their traditional knowledge unused in a world of concrete and pavement

*Connection: Forced dispersal from ancestral territories - both bears and humans scattered by changing conditions*

### 3. Twenty years before extinction
**Species:** Pregnant females unable to find suitable denning sites as rain melts traditional snow caves, newborns freezing in flooded dens

**Human:** Anyu teaches ice-hunting techniques online to urban Inuit youth who will never touch sea ice, preserving knowledge for a world that no longer exists

*Connection: Reproductive failure - next generation cannot inherit what previous generations built their lives upon*

### 4. Five years before extinction
**Species:** 200 bears remain in captive breeding programs, unable to reproduce successfully without ice hunting behaviors to trigger natural cycles

**Human:** Anyu holds her grandfather's ice chisel, running her thumb over symbols that once meant survival, now meaningless as hieroglyphs

*Connection: Cultural artifacts outlasting their function - tools and knowledge becoming museum pieces while their creators still live*

## Elegiac Quality
Bears witness to parallel extinctions without sentimentality - the quiet dignity of species and culture facing impossible odds. Mourns the loss of foundation itself, not just what was built upon it.
