# Staghorn coral / Carmen Delgado-Ruiz

## Species Arc: Staghorn coral
2035: Functional extinction recorded across Caribbean range. Final population census: zero viable colonies. Water temperature: 31.8°C sustained for 127 days. Bleaching threshold exceeded by 1.8°C. Symbiotic zooxanthellae expelled. Calcium carbonate framework dissolution rate: 0.3mm annually. Three-dimensional reef architecture collapsed to rubble field. Ecosystem services terminated. 600 dependent species: habitat loss confirmed.

## Human Arc: Carmen Delgado-Ruiz
Carmen floats at dawn, thermometer reading 31.2°C. The fever behind her eyes matches the ocean's. Three people at yesterday's community meeting. Her daughters' voices on the phone grow more distant. The metal nursery framework stands empty beneath her, built to last centuries. She thinks of her husband's careful temperature logs, how he believed in numbers. The last coral fragment loses its color as she watches.

## Parallel Moments

### 1. Eighteen months before extinction
**Species:** Ocean temperature exceeds 30°C threshold for 45 consecutive days. Mass bleaching event begins across 80% of remaining colonies.

**Human:** Carmen installs the last shade structure over the community nursery, her hands shaking as she reads 30.1°C on her husband's thermometer.

*Connection: Both cross fatal temperature thresholds simultaneously - the corals expelling their symbiotic partners, Carmen realizing her community project cannot survive the heat.*

### 2. One year before extinction
**Species:** Colony fragmentation accelerates. Remaining staghorn structures lose architectural integrity, unable to maintain 3D reef framework.

**Human:** Community meetings dwindle from 30 people to 8. Carmen watches neighbors pack their belongings, the collective restoration dream fragmenting.

*Connection: Structural collapse - both coral colonies and human communities lose the critical mass needed to maintain their foundational architecture.*

### 3. Six months before extinction
**Species:** Final reproductive event fails. Water too warm for successful spawning. No new polyp recruitment recorded.

**Human:** Carmen's daughters leave for New York. No new volunteers join the restoration project. The next generation abandons the reef.

*Connection: Reproductive failure - neither species nor community can sustain the next generation in the changed environment.*

### 4. Three months before extinction
**Species:** Last viable colony fragments detected in deepest refugia. Calcium carbonate skeleton begins dissolution in acidified water.

**Human:** Carmen develops heat exhaustion, spending entire days in water trying to shade the final coral fragments with her own body.

*Connection: Final sacrifice - both species and human exhaust their last reserves attempting to protect what remains.*

### 5. Final week
**Species:** Temperature sensors record 31.8°C. Last living tissue separates from skeleton. Symbiotic relationship terminates after millions of years.

**Human:** Carmen floats motionless above the bleached cemetery, fever matching the ocean's temperature, thinking: 'We were never meant to survive alone.'

*Connection: Dissolution of partnership - the ancient coral-algae symbiosis ends as Carmen realizes human isolation mirrors ecological breakdown.*

## Elegiac Quality
The narrative achieves memorial through precise documentation rather than sentimentality - recording exact temperatures, population numbers, and personal details with the same scientific attention. Loss becomes sacred through specificity: Carmen's husband's thermometer, the exact degree of temperature rise, the dwindling meeting attendance. Both extinctions are witnessed with dignity, their interconnection emerging through parallel structure rather than explicit metaphor.
