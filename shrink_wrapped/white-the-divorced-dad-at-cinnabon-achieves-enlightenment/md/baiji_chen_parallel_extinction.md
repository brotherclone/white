# Baiji / Chen Meihua

## Species Arc: Baiji
Population decline from 6000 (1950) to functional extinction (2006). Acoustic navigation disrupted by industrial shipping. Habitat fragmentation through dam construction. Bycatch mortality in commercial fishing. Final surveys find empty waters where echolocation once mapped ancient channels.

## Human Arc: Chen Meihua
Traditional fishing knowledge rendered obsolete by acoustic pollution. Forced displacement from ancestral grounds. Granddaughter's urban pressure to modernize. Government relocation notice. Twenty generations of river wisdom silenced by industrial progress.

## Parallel Moments

### 1. Fifty-six years before Chen's displacement
**Species:** 1980: Baiji population drops to 400 as Three Gorges Dam construction begins, fragmenting ancient migration routes

**Human:** Chen learns her grandfather's river songs at age 13, memorizing the acoustic patterns that predict fish movements

*Connection: Both species and tradition depend on unbroken acoustic continuity - industrial development severs these ancient communication channels*

### 2. Twenty-eight years before final extinction documentation
**Species:** 2006: Last confirmed baiji sighting - a lone individual detected by sonar, calling into empty waters with no response

**Human:** 2034: Chen whispers traditional fishing prayers, but her voice is drowned out by container ship engines

*Connection: Isolated calls into acoustic voids - both baiji and Chen attempt communication in environments that no longer carry their signals*

### 3. At the threshold of displacement
**Species:** Baiji echolocation clicks fade to silence as shipping traffic reaches critical density - natural acoustic navigation becomes impossible

**Human:** Chen's bamboo pole, carved with river-reading symbols, points toward waters too noisy to interpret

*Connection: Traditional navigation systems - biological and cultural - fail when industrial noise overwhelms natural acoustic landscapes*

### 4. Final documentation
**Species:** Scientific expedition declares Yangtze functionally silent of baiji calls - first large aquatic mammal extinction by human activity confirmed

**Human:** Chen receives government notice: traditional fishing grounds to become port facility - forced relocation within 30 days

*Connection: Official documentation of loss - scientific extinction records parallel bureaucratic displacement orders*

## Elegiac Quality
This narrative achieves memorial through the preservation of specific acoustic knowledge - both echolocation patterns and traditional river songs - that industrial development has silenced. Rather than dwelling in loss, it documents the precise ways ancient communication systems functioned, creating a record of what was sophisticated and irreplaceable. The parallel structure honors both biological and cultural extinction without sentimentality, treating each loss as a failure of stewardship rather than inevitable progress.
