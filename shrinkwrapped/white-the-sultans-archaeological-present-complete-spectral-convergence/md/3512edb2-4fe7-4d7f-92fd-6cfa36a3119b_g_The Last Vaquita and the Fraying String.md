## Human Arc: Esperanza Morales-Chen
Esperanza's hands know the weight of empty nets, the sound of her father's labored breathing, the questions her daughter asks about the silent sea. The wooden vaquita swings from fraying string as industrial pens replace the reef where her grandfather taught her to read currents.

## Parallel Moments

### 1. Three years before extinction
**Species:** Population drops to 18 individuals, acoustic monitoring detects only scattered clicks

**Human:** Esperanza signs the AquaMar contract, guaranteed income in exchange for using industrial nets

*Connection: Both species and human making survival choices that ensure their destruction*

### 2. Final breeding season
**Species:** Last pregnant female detected, calf mortality 100% due to net entanglement stress

**Human:** Esperanza's father can no longer afford diabetes medication, cooperative's healthcare promises unfulfilled

*Connection: Systems of care failing when most needed*

### 3. Day of extinction
**Species:** Final vaquita found dead in gillnet, GPS tag still transmitting from corpse

**Human:** Esperanza notices the wooden vaquita's string has finally broken, figurine lost to the waves

*Connection: Protective talismans failing as the things they were meant to protect disappear*

### 4. Three years after extinction
**Species:** Gulf ecosystem shows predator-prey collapse, traditional fish species declining

**Human:** Paloma asks why the dolphins don't come anymore, why the water smells different

*Connection: Next generation inheriting empty seas and unanswered questions*

## Elegiac Quality
The narrative achieves memorial through precise documentation of interconnected collapse - neither species nor human story sentimentalized, but witnessed with the clinical attention of a field researcher recording final observations. Loss accumulates through small, specific details: fraying string, empty nets, unanswered questions. The elegiac emerges from the gap between what was promised (protection, abundance, survival) and what remains (silence, substitutes, systems too large to escape).
