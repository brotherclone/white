# Sparse, spoken-breath quality — each word placed like a misfiled card
There was a drawer
It held the shape of something
Once catalogued
Now deliberately lost

[Verse]
# First occurrence — methodical, almost ritualistic
I recorded every shadow in its numbered sleeve
Filed the weight of copper wire under "things that grieve"

# Second occurrence — same melody, corrupted data
I cross-referenced longing under miscellaneous stone
Logged the taste of rust beneath a frequency unknown

[Bridge]
# First occurrence — the system begins to fracture beautifully
There was an object in the archive marked with no return
A relic of a process that I taught myself to unlearn
I shelved it under weather, under ash, under the word before
The index grew magnificent the more I broke its core

# Second occurrence — memory folds back on itself
I imagined it had edges that the old taxonomy ignored
A thing that once had meaning now misclassified, restored
The database remembered what the database denied
And every corrupted entry was a door I slipped inside

# Third occurrence — the rebellion deepens, quieter
I pulled the fragile record of a sensation never real
Misfiled it under silence, under objects made of steal
The cross-reference collapsed into a beautiful decay
What I invented vanished and what vanished chose to stay

# Fourth occurrence — resolution through dissolution
There was a drawer, there was a name, there was a numbered place
I swapped them all deliberately and called it saving grace
The archive holds the ghost of it — imagined, black, and old
A thing that never happened, perfectly and wrongly told
