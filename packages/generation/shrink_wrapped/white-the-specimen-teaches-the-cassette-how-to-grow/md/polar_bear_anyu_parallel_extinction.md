# Polar bear / Anyu Kanguq

## Species Arc: Polar bear
Population decline from 25,000 stable (2001) to functional extinction (2100). Arctic sea ice habitat loss accelerates beyond species adaptation capacity. Ringed seal hunting grounds disappear. Maternity den sites flood with rain-on-snow events. Swimming distances between ice floes exceed metabolic capacity. Captive breeding programs maintain genetic material but cannot restore wild populations.

## Human Arc: Anyu Kanguq
Traditional knowledge keeper watches ancestral wisdom become obsolete. Ice-reading skills passed down through generations fail as Arctic transforms beyond recognition. GPS replaces grandfather's ivory pick. Last traditional hunt in 2043. Hangs up hunting gear. Tells granddaughter: 'The ice doesn't speak our language anymore.'

## Parallel Moments

### 1. Fifty-seven years before extinction
**Species:** 2043: Arctic sea ice extent reaches historic low, 40% below 1980s average

**Human:** 2043: Anyu carries GPS beacons on her last traditional hunt, her grandfather's ice pick useless

*Connection: Traditional navigation systems failing as the fundamental landscape disappears*

### 2. Thirty years before extinction
**Species:** 2070: Polar bears swim 200+ kilometers between ice floes, cubs drowning from exhaustion

**Human:** 2070: Anyu's granddaughter learns climate science instead of ice-reading, ancestral knowledge ending

*Connection: Survival skills that sustained life for millennia becoming death sentences*

### 3. Seven years before extinction
**Species:** 2093: Last wild maternity den documented, flooded by unseasonable rain

**Human:** 2093: Anyu's village relocates inland as coastal permafrost collapses, hunting grounds lost forever

*Connection: Safe spaces for reproduction and renewal destroyed by environmental chaos*

### 4. Year of extinction
**Species:** 2100: Final wild polar bear dies alone on a fragment of ice the size of a kitchen table

**Human:** 2100: Anyu's ivory pick is donated to a museum, labeled 'obsolete Arctic navigation tool'

*Connection: The end of ancient relationships between species and place, knowledge and survival*

## Elegiac Quality
Bears witness to the end of ancient relationships between species and place without romanticizing loss. Documents the quiet catastrophe of traditional knowledge becoming obsolete alongside species extinction. Honors both scientific data and indigenous wisdom as equally valid ways of understanding disappearance.
