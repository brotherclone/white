## Production Notes
- Use actual field recordings from 1980s New Jersey suburban environments
- Layer in distant Misfits-style backing vocals on the choruses  
- The bridge should sound like it's being told in a teacher's lounge
- Include subtle sound of actual fondue bubbling underneath throughout
- Tempo should be driving but slightly off-kilter, like a memory that doesn't quite line up

## Thematic Analysis
This represents perfect Orange album territory: PAST + THING + IMAGINED. The Fondue Club exists in that space where childhood overhearing adult concerns creates a mythology that may or may not be true. The broken memory palace mechanics are in full effect - the geography has changed (A&P â†’ CVS), the context has shifted (pre-internet suburban isolation), but the uncanny residue remains.

The Misfits reference serves as temporal anchor - they're the "real" band that made it out of the same scene, while the Fondue Club represents the kids who disappeared into suburban legend. Perfect rebracketing material: taking whispered teacher concerns and adolescent rebellion and transforming them into something between horror story and nostalgic elegy.
